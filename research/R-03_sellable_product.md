# R-03 — Design and Sell a Tech Product

**Status:** candidate — not active (Nick flagged as "maybe, eventually")
**Fit:** highest ceiling of the three, and the only one where Nick's
actual competitive advantages — CAD, 3D printing, mechanical design,
regulatory/chemical engineering background — do the earning.

## What it is
Design something once, sell it repeatedly: printed mechanical products
or kits (the workshop already produces cycloidal gearboxes and
actuator housings — adjacent niches include robotics kits, shop
fixtures, specialty organizers), digital design files (STLs sold on
marketplaces — zero fulfillment), or a small software/hardware tool.
The recurring theme: productize a byproduct of engineering Nick is
already doing anyway.

## Honest math
Unlike R-01/R-02, returns aren't capped by deployed capital — they're
capped by whether anyone wants the thing. Most first products sell
approximately zero units; a product that finds a real niche can clear
more in a month than R-01 clears in a year at this capital level.
High variance, but it's variance on effort (recoverable, educational)
rather than on capital (gone).

## Effort / capital / risk
- **Effort:** heavy and front-loaded — design, listing, iteration; the
  real cost is that these hours compete directly with CEV build hours
  (though product engineering doubles as CEV skill practice)
- **Capital:** low — filament, maybe a listing fee; well under $100 to
  test a digital-files-first approach
- **Main risks:** no demand (most likely outcome for attempt #1),
  fulfillment time creep if physical, marketplace fees/policies
- **Ceiling:** the only uncapped one of the three

## First build if selected
A shortlist of 5–10 candidate products ranked by (a) overlap with
work already done for CEV, (b) evidence of existing demand, and
(c) fulfillment burden — digital-first preferred. No building until
the shortlist survives a demand check.
