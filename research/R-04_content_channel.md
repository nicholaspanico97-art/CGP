# R-04 — Build Documentation / Content Channel

**Status:** candidate — Nick interested (Jul 18, 2026)
**Fit:** near-zero marginal cost (the builds are happening anyway);
the demand engine R-03 needs; compounds instead of paying hourly.

## What it is
Publicly document the workshop's engineering: printed cycloidal
gearboxes now, the SCP actuator program ([R-05](R-05_scp_actuators.md))
later. YouTube + Printables/blog writeups. Direct revenue (ads,
sponsors) is slow and unlikely early; the real return is an audience
that already watched the thing get built when it goes on sale.

## Ground rules (Nick, Jul 18)
- **Bee is entirely out of frame.** The channel covers the engineering,
  never the why. Scope check before anything is published.
- Nick has also considered a separate CNS/psychedelic-themed channel.
  If pursued, it is a SEPARATE channel (audiences don't overlap; mixed
  channels hurt discovery) and it is a passion project, not a CGP
  workstream — psychedelic content is monetization-fragile (science /
  harm-reduction framing survives; use-promotion framing gets
  demonetized). Not tracked further here.

## Honest math
Maker channels take 1–2 years and consistent output to reach any
direct money. Treat direct revenue as $0 for planning. Value = R-03
demand channel + skills (documentation quality also improves the
sellable build guides themselves).

## Effort / risk
- **Effort:** the real cost — filming/editing competes with build
  hours. Mitigation: document-as-you-go (photos, short clips, narrated
  timelapses) rather than produced videos at first.
- **Risk:** minimal capital; main risk is time sink with no audience.
  Kill criterion worth setting: if 10 pieces of content produce ~zero
  traction, drop to write-ups only.
