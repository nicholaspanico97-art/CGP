## How this track works

Ad hoc opportunity-hunting, done with discipline:

1. Nick spots an opportunity (or asks FAT to research a market/asset).
2. FAT provides analysis: pros, cons, historical context, data checks,
   risk framing. **FAT never renders a verdict or a buy/sell call —
   the decision is Nick's, always** (Standing Rule 2 + hard assistant
   boundary).
3. Before any entry: the journal entry is written FIRST — thesis,
   position size, max loss, exit conditions. If it's not written down
   before the order, the trade doesn't happen.
4. Nick executes personally. Outcome and lesson get recorded in
   `JOURNAL.md` win or lose, and the capital ledger in PROJECT_STATE.md
   is updated.

## Pre-entry checklist (all boxes or no trade)

- [ ] Thesis stated in one or two sentences — what do I believe that
      the market currently doesn't price, and why do I get to know it?
- [ ] Named downside: what specifically makes this thesis wrong, and
      how would I notice?
- [ ] Position size within cap (below)
- [ ] Max acceptable loss in dollars written down
- [ ] Exit conditions decided: profit target and/or invalidation level
      and/or time stop — chosen NOW, not after entry
- [ ] Not revenge-trading a recent loss; not FOMO on a move already made
- [ ] Venue/custody risk considered (exchange, counterparty, chain)

## Journal discipline (the non-negotiable part)

Every trade gets a journal entry — before entry and after exit. An
untracked trade is a rule violation even if it wins. The journal is the
product of this track: after 10–20 entries it will say, in Nick's own
handwriting, whether discretionary trading is making or losing money
here and why.
